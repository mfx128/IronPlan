Incline Smith Machine Press

4×6-8

Why: Upper chest shelf is the #1 visual marker of a big chest. Retract scapula hard before unracking — blades down and back the whole set.

Sub: Incline DB Press or Incline Barbell Press.

Tempo: 3-1-1-0180s Rest

Flat DB Press

4×10

Form: Get a deep stretch at the bottom — elbows just below bench level. Do NOT clank the DBs at the top. Stop 2 inches short and squeeze hard.

Sub: Flat Barbell Bench Press.

Tempo: 3-0-1-090s Rest

Low-to-High Cable Fly

3×12-15

Why: Cables set low to pull upward targets the upper pec fibers post-press — maximizes the chest-to-shoulder tie-in that makes you look "full" in a t-shirt. Hold the squeeze 1s at peak.

Sub: High-to-Low Fly for lower pec, or Pec Deck Machine.

Tempo: 2-0-1-260s Rest

Weighted Dips

3×10-12

Form: Lean chest forward, chin tucked. This is a chest/lower-pec exercise here — if you go upright it shifts to shoulders. Adds the bottom of the chest that makes the pec look "dropped."

Tempo: 3-1-1-0120s Rest

Rope Pushdowns

4×15

Form: Split the rope apart at the bottom to hit the lateral head — the "horseshoe" shape visible from behind. Keep elbows pinned to sides. Hinge slightly at the waist.

Tempo: 2-0-1-160s Rest

Overhead DB Tricep Extension

3×12

Why: Long head of the tricep — the part that creates arm thickness when viewed from the side. Only fully activated when the arm is overhead. Deep stretch at the bottom every rep.

Sub: Cable Overhead Extension or Skullcrushers.

Tempo: 3-0-1-160s Rest

Weighted Pull-Ups

4×6-10

Why: Nothing builds lat width (the V-taper) like weighted pull-ups. Full dead hang at the bottom every rep — no partial reps. Drive elbows down to your hips, not just pulling with hands.

Sub: Heavy Lat Pulldowns (pronated grip, wide).

Tempo: 2-1-1-0180s Rest

Barbell Bent-Over Row

4×8-10

Form: 45° torso, overhand grip. Pull to lower chest/upper navel — that's mid-back thickness. No jerking. If your hips rise to move the weight, strip a plate. Back density is what makes you look thick from the side.

Sub: T-Bar Row (chest-supported) or Pendlay Rows.

Tempo: 2-0-1-1120s Rest

Single-Arm Cable Pulldown

3×12

Form: Sit sideways to the stack, allow full overhead stretch. Unilateral work catches lat imbalances. Focus on feeling the stretch, then driving the elbow to your hip pocket. Adds the "flare" when relaxed.

Sub: Neutral-Grip Lat Pulldown (bilateral).

Tempo: 3-1-1-090s Rest

Chest-Supported DB Row

3×10-12

Form: Chest on the incline pad eliminates lower back fatigue and momentum — every rep is pure mid-back. Drive elbows straight back and squeeze shoulder blades together hard at the top.

Tempo: 2-0-1-290s Rest

Straight Arm Pulldown

3×15

Form: Slight hip hinge, arms straight but soft at the elbows. Pure lat isolation — biceps are completely out of the movement. Finishes the lat pump before moving to arms.

Tempo: 2-0-1-160s Rest

Incline DB Curl

3×12

Why: Arm behind the body = long head of the bicep on stretch. That's the peak. Supinate (rotate palm up) fully as you curl. Don't let the elbows swing forward.

Sub: Spider Curls on incline bench.

Tempo: 3-0-1-160s Rest

Hammer Curls

3×12

Why: Neutral grip targets the brachialis — the muscle that sits under the bicep and pushes it up. The biggest factor in arm thickness. Alternate arms, no swinging. Keep elbows pinned.

Tempo: 2-0-1-160s Rest

Hack Squat

4×10-12

Form: Low, close foot placement to maximally hit the outer quad sweep — the teardrop shape. Go deep past 90°. No knee lockout at the top. This is the primary quad mass builder today.

Sub: Pendulum Squat or Leg Press (low narrow stance).

Tempo: 3-1-1-0180s Rest

Leg Press (High Volume)

3×15

Form: After the hack squat, volume work on the press floods legs with blood and metabolic stress. Don't lock knees at the top. Feet shoulder-width, mid-platform. On the last set, do a drop set immediately to failure.

Tempo: 2-1-1-090s Rest

Leg Extension

4×15

Intensity: Squeeze hard at the top for 1 second. On the final set, do a triple drop set — reduce weight twice, going to failure each time. Burns the quads completely out before posterior chain work.

Tempo: 2-1-1-060s Rest

Romanian Deadlift (Barbell)

4×8-10

Form: Hinge at hips, bar drags close to legs, soft knee bend. Go until you feel a deep hamstring stretch — most people stop way too early. Squeeze glutes to drive back up. 3-second negative.

Sub: DB RDL or Single-Leg RDL (add load as you progress).

Tempo: 3-1-1-0180s Rest

Lying Leg Curl

3×12

Form: Hips stay pinned flat — if they raise, the weight is too heavy. Pause for 1 second at peak contraction. Finishes hamstrings after RDLs to complete the posterior chain work. Toes pointed slightly outward to hit biceps femoris.

Tempo: 3-1-1-060s Rest

Standing Calf Raise

4×12

Form: Go heavy. Kill the Achilles reflex with a strict 2-second pause at the bottom stretch. Calves respond to both heavy loads AND high volume — this is the heavy day. Drive up explosively.

Tempo: 2-2-1-160s Rest

Seated Calf Raise

3×20

Why: Knee bent isolates the soleus — the lower, deeper calf muscle that gives calves their width. Lighter weight, full stretch, full pump. Squeeze to failure on last set.

Tempo: 2-2-1-145s Rest

Seated DB Overhead Press

4×8-10

Form: Back supported, core tight, avoid excessive arch. Stop just short of lockout — keeps tension on the delt. This is the shoulder mass builder. Drive the weight with intent.

Sub: Standing Barbell Military Press or Smith Machine Press.

Tempo: 2-0-1-0120s Rest

Cable Lateral Raise + Rest-Pause

4×15

Why: This is the single most important exercise if you want to look bigger. Medial delt width literally adds inches to your frame. Cable behind the back for constant tension. Pull OUT not up. Traps completely relaxed. On the last 2 sets: rest-pause — hit 15, rest 15s, go to failure again.

Sub: DB Lateral Raise (lean slightly away from the cable stack).

Tempo: 2-0-1-160s Rest

Reverse Pec Deck (Rear Delt)

3×15-20

Why: Rear delts create the 3D "capped" shoulder look and fill out the upper back. Keep traps 100% relaxed. If your traps are working, the weight is too heavy. Pinch shoulder blades at peak.

Sub: Bent-Over DB Rear Delt Fly or Cable Face Pull variation.

Tempo: 2-0-1-260s Rest

Face Pulls

3×20

Form: Rope to forehead level, externally rotate at peak — pull hands apart like you're showing off a double bicep. Protects shoulder health long-term. Non-negotiable if you're pressing heavy.

Tempo: 2-0-1-245s Rest

EZ Bar Curl ↔ Skullcrusher

4×10-12

Form: Superset — same bar, same bench. Curls: elbows pinned to sides, no body english. Skullcrushers: lower bar to forehead slowly, elbows stay pointed at ceiling. Arms are 2/3 of your upper arm — training them together with heavy compound moves is the most efficient path to sleeve-filling arms.

Tempo: 2-0-1-190s After Pair

Cable Curl ↔ Rope Pushdown

3×12-15

Form: Superset Finisher. Cable keeps tension at the bottom of the curl where DBs go slack. Pushdowns: split rope at bottom for lateral head detail. Pump maximum blood into both muscle groups to finish. The skin should feel tight.

Tempo: 2-0-1-160s After Pair
